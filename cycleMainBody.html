Water is Precious

Did you know that water is a vital ingredient for life on earth, and that we share it with every other living thing on the planet? As humans, we borrow water from nature to use in our homes, schools, and workplaces, but we always have to send it back to the environment, so that it can be used by other living creatures as well.

Precipitation

Precipitation is when water falls from the sky as rain, snow, or hail. It's an important part of the water cycle. After it falls, it can end up in rivers, lakes, or the ground. Scientists study this to see how much and where it rains or snows on Earth. It's like nature's way of sharing water with everything!

Evaporation

Evaporation is when water turns into vapor and rises into the sky. Think of it like water's way of climbing up to become part of a cloud. It happens when water gets warm, like when the sun heats up lakes and oceans.

Condensation

Condensation is when the water vapor in the sky cools down and becomes tiny water droplets, forming clouds. Imagine it as water's way of grouping together in the sky, getting ready to fall back down as rain or snow later on. It's like the clouds are getting ready for a water party!
